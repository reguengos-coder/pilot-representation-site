The Importance of Pilot Advocacy and Union Representation

The International Pilots Representation Group (IPRG) is a global organization committed to advocating for the rights, working conditions, and professional interests of pilots worldwide.

Why Pilots Need Representation

Pilots play a critical role in ensuring the safety and efficiency of global air travel, yet they often find themselves subject to unfair labor practices, unstable contracts, and corporate decisions that undermine their job security.

  • Unilateral contract changes that reduce benefits and increase workloads.
  • Insufficient rest policies, leading to fatigue and safety concerns.
  • Pay disparities and inconsistent compensation structures.
  • Retaliation for voicing safety concerns.

The Role of Unions in Protecting Pilots

Historically, unions have played a pivotal role in improving the working conditions of pilots.

  • Collective Bargaining Power: Ensuring pilots receive fair pay, benefits, and work-life balance.
  • Legal Protection: Representation in disputes involving airlines or regulatory authorities.
  • Job Security: Preventing unfair dismissals and contractual violations.
  • Safety Advocacy: Addressing critical issues like pilot fatigue, mental health, and training quality.

The Mission of IPRG

The International Pilots Representation Group (IPRG) aims to unify pilots across the globe, offering a collective voice to address common concerns and establish industry-wide standards.
